2022: Reconnecting with Friends

This end-of-year letter, also available in Portuguese, is part of a series started in 2000. Previous editions are found here.

Pleease reply to jamaral@ualberta.ca.

Our after-Christimas time in 2021 was split with first Scott going on one more of his intrepid skiing trips right before the new year, and then I spent a few days in Calgary with my friend Alex. I was due to go to Brazil for a long-delayed visit to Belo Horizonte, but both Alex and I caught COVID during our trip to Calgary. Thus, the trip to Brazil was once more postponed at the last minute.

In January I stepped down from my position as a member of the Board of Directors of the Standard Performance Evaluation Corporation (SPEC) in which I had served for many years. I was awarded the SPEC Presidential Award in recognition of the many service roles in which I contributed to SPEC.

With the changes to the travel plans, Junior kept enjoying more long Winter walks to the dog park. I am often found talking with and petting all kinds of dogs in the dog park. Junior is often referred to as "the happiest dog" and some call me "the dog whisperer." Sometimes Junior would invite a friend to join us. Ricardo, a friend from Mexico, misses his dogs while in Edmonton and thus was keen to join us.

In March we managed to go out for lunch with our new friends Dmitri and Olga. They are the parents of my former M.Sc. student Artem Chikin. They were visiting Artem's brother and nephews in Edmonton last Fall when the Russian war against Ukraine started. The combination of Dmitri's past career with the USSR army and his public loud criticism of Putin made it impossible for them to return to Russia without severe consequences. Thus, they requested asylum in Canada. Through them, we had a first-row perspective on what was happening in Russia and with the people of Russia throughout this war. In our conversations, we learned about Dmitri and Olga's interest in classical music.

Later in March I finally managed to take my first post-COVID trip and my first stop was in Porto Alegre to see my Mom. We enjoyed spending many days together. We drank lots of Chimarrao and did some cooking together. My friend Daniel Petry was the one to pick me up at the airport and help me rediscover Porto Alegre. I went to my first live-music concert in years, seeing Ney Lisboa returning to the stage. Daniel Petry also took me to a great stairway live samba in downtown Porto Alegre. At my Mom's place, we had a nice gathering with my nephews and nieces from the Urtassum side of my family. I got to see my sister Salete and my friend Rogerio for the first time in a while and managed to squeeze in a happy hour with our friends Ney and Karin.

From Porto Alegre, I went to Florianopolis to seat down, drink beer, and catch up with my great friend Horacio, who I had not seen in many many years. Horacio is one of my big-life brothers with whom I feel as if we said goodbye yesterday when I see him again after years apart. It was a great opportunity to go to my aunt Lucia's house for a traditional Churrasco Gaucho. In Florianopolis, I also reconnected with my friend Jader and met his partner for the first time. The shortstop in Florianopolis was very intense with opportunities to reconnect with dear friends.

I made my way to Belo Horizonte where I delivered my workshop on how to present research ideas and results. This is a workshop that has been received with great success in Brazil. At the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais (UFMG) they wrote an article about this workshop. Colleagues from Sao Paulo, Niteroi, and other places noticed and I ended up returning to Brazil in September. The students gave me the best Mineira hospitality organizing a happy hour both for my arrival and for my departure. When Daniel heard that they had organized a party for my arrival he said "I did not know that you were that important." Having discovered how much I was missing live music performances, I went seeking concerts in Belo Horizonte. I saw Joao Bosco, a well-known MPB composer and performed, and Jorge Aragao, a great samba composer, delivered great performances. I also made my way to the Clube da Esquina to see a nice band from Belo Horizonte called Get Beattles. My host, Fernando Pereira took me out for lunch to a typical Mineiro restaurant with his family.

My final stop in Brazil was São Paulo where I spent most of the time hanging out and going out with my dear friends Fred and Gloria who had come from Santos to spend time with me. We managed to eat lunch at the highly-sought-out Tordesilhas restaurant and also enjoyed great pizza for dinner. São Paulo is one of the best places in the world to eat great pizzas. I even got to meet Gloria and Fred's godson, Gabriel, an impressive young man working with social programs in favelas in Rio de Janeiro. Gabriel was very engaged in our conversations and I was pleased to learn about his worldview.

Upon my return from Brazil, with my renovated interest in live music performance, Scott and I invited Dmitri and Olga to join us to go to a concert by the Edmonton Symphonic Orchestra. They were very positively impressed by the quality of the ESO performance, and by the superb Winspear concert hall in Edmonton. Given all the loss and tumult in their lives, the live music experience brought tears to their eyes. We followed that with other live music performances during the year, including a concert given by the organist of London's St. Paul Catedral in the phenomenal Davis Organ at the Winspear, a Symphony-Under-The-Sky performance at Hawrelak park, and a December mixed performance by the orchestra at Concordia University. While attending the organ concert we met with our friend Reva and she recommended that during my upcoming trip to San Diego I should check out the Sunday concert of the Spreckels Organ Pavilion, the largest outdoor musical instrument in the world, at the Balboa Park when I was there. Thus, this was the program when I reencountered Walter Bays, a colleague from my days as a member of the Board of Directors for the Standard Performance Evaluation Corporation (SPEC).

This year marked the end of a long run of great soccer and championships by Daniel's Windsor Park Community League Soccer team as the boys reached the age at which they can no longer play youth soccer. They finished their career by winning the City of Edmonton Championship in their age and category -- the only trophy that they had not conquered until now. We had a great time celebrating the season with the now young man and with the great group of parents and coaches that supported them through many seasons.

In June I had an opportunity to wear my academic regalia once again to attend the graduation where our friend Michael Phair received an honorary doctorate degree from the University of Alberta. Michael Phair is a very important figure in the LGBTQ2S community in Edmonton. He was the first openly gay member of the Edmonton City Council. He has also served as the chair of the University of Alberta board in the past.

In June we took a trip to Calgary to visit with Daniel's sisters. Mikaela has a baby daughter now. We also enjoyed visiting with Emily's family. During this trip, we had time to go out for dinner to catch up with our friends Carolina, Shelly, and their son Seba.

The road tour of the Broadway show Hamilton stopped at the Jubilee Auditorium in Edmonton, which is two blocks away from our house. Daniel agreed to join his old men to see a great performance.

I had several opportunities to enjoy performances by the Edmonton Ballet, managed by our friend Sheri Summerville. Matt and Fabiola joined me for a performance where we met Diego Ramalho and Samuel Ramos, two Brazilian dancers in the company. The company is growing in popularity and is presenting great shows. In December we were lucky to win a raffle of a trip for two to go to Ottawa to see the Edmonton Ballet perform at the National Arts Centre in March 2023. We are looking very much forward to it.

My research lab was very busy during the Summer with a record number of undergraduate students preparing material for the Fall teaching and getting started on their research careers. For social events, Junior invited them for a walk in the dog park, and I organized some of the now-traditional cooking-together parties. For these events, I design a menu and gather the equipment and ingredients so that the guests can do all the cooking together. We enjoyed the great weather while cooking outside.

When we go on walks or take him to the off-leash dog park, Junior has a great preference for women, especially for young girls that speak with him in a high voice saying how cute or friendly he is. So, one day, during a social gathering organized by the Windsor Park Community League, Junior was in heaven when he was surrounded by a group of young girls.

My department has a tradition of organizing board game parties. It is a great way for students and faculty to gather and to have something to do right away when they meet. Groups grab a board game and seat around a table to play. I organized the first Games party after the pandemic in July. We were lucky to have an evening with very nice weather and with no wind. Thus, this turned out to be the first Games party with game tables out in the backyard. At the end of the evening, students were anxiously asking when the next game's party would take place.

If you are wondering what Scott has been during in the middle of my travels and these parties it is time to reveal: he is taking every opportunity that he has to go off to the mountains for skiing or for hiking. Scott has gotten involved with several groups of people that are very interested in these activities. Scott posts interesting trip reports in his FB stream along with pictures. In August he even received a visit from Stuart Howe of the Howe's the Hike youtube fame. Thus, Scott ended up feature in some Howe's videos. However, in August their plans for a long hike were frustrated by global warming. The unusually higher temperatures in the mountains resulted in much more snow and ice melting leading to small creeks becoming unpassable roaring rivers, and the wildfires made the air in the mountains unbreathable for a while. Thus, they were back earlier than expected from the mountains and we turn lemons into lemonade by organizing a last-minute successful backyard party.

Instead of our traditional large August backyard party, this year we had several smaller gatherings. In July we organized a Bruschette & Drinks Neighborhood Open House. It was great to gather known and new neighbors for visiting that extended later into the evening.

To celebrate his 18th birthday, Daniel asked if he could "borrow" some of our friends. He picked a few of the people that he knew from our parties to come and have dinner out on the deck and then some great time around a fire that he set up out on our driveway. It turned out to be a great evening where our neighbor Neil connected with our friend John Shaw and with Scott to go on more challenging hikes. Neil had been my companion to going to the theatre earlier in the year when Scott was out hiking.

We were fortunate to reconnect with our great friends Fran and Steve who have now lived in Brisbane in Australia for many years. We had visits by Steve and Fran at different times. I even had the joy of playing uncle to Sidney, their first granddaughter. Daniel also enjoyed very much visiting with Fran. When Daniel was little Fran was often his "Mom" in Edmonton.

September 11th marked the 30th anninversary since Scott and I met for the first time, at a lesbian bar named Chances in Austin, Texas. At the last minute, Scott decided to gather some friends over for a celebration. He managed to gather a nice group of friends for a backyard celebration and we even managed to take a couple of nice pictures to mark the date.

Robina is the daughter of my Ph.D. supervisor, Joydeep Ghosh, in Austin, and Tito is from Rio Grande do Sul in Brazil and completed his Ph.D. at The University of Texas at Austin. Tito and Robina becoming a couple and then Tito taking up a faculty position at the University of Alberta is one of these great coincidences of life. We have enjoyed very much getting acquainted with Tito and getting reacquainted with Robina now as an adult. I was having dinner with them at a restaurant in March when I got a call that Scott's emergency beacon had been activated while he was skiing in the backcountry. It turned out that Scott had dislocated his shoulder and needed a helicopter airlift and a trip to the hospital in an ambulance after spending a night in pain in the middle of a pine forest somewhere between Jasper and Banff. He was fine as soon as his shoulder was put back in place at the hospital.

Among the many gatherings in our backyard during the Summer, I managed to organize one for several colleagues from the University of Alberta, including some who have served with me in many of my governance roles at the university. This was a good way to mark my transition from being super involved with many high-level governance committees at the university to disconnecting from all of them. From July 1st, 2022 to June 30th, 2023 I am on sabbatical and thus, instead of my usual teaching and governance work, I have been enjoying a much stronger connection with my industrial research partners.

I returned to Brazil in September. This time to deliver my workshop at the University of São Paulo (USP) where I was very well received by my colleague Alfredo Goldman. The students at USP enjoyed the workshop. While I was in São Paulo my friend Daniel Petry was arriving in São Paulo to take up his new position as a faculty member at USP. Thus, we had a great opportunity to reconnect again. I also reconnected once again with my friend Marcio, who I had also met with Fred and Gloria during my April visit. The weather was not the greatest for my visit to São Paulo: there was cold weather and rain and São Paulo is not the greatest city to move around on rainy days. I managed to include a one-day visit to Unicamp to reconnect in person with my colleague and friend Guido Araujo. After São Paulo I few to Porto Alegre to give a talk at a conference. There I saw many colleagues that I had not seen in a while and went to a very nice dinner at a restaurant named Raven.

In October, while attending a conference in Chicago, we had a coincidence of having four Brazilian researchers around a table during one of the presentations: (from left) Mauricio Breternitz (ISTAR ISCTE, Portugal), Alfredo Goldman (USP, Brazil), me, José Moreira (IBM, USA). It was in Chicago that I consolidated my reputation with Alfredo about picking up good places to eat --- after doing very well in Porto Alegre. Thus, when we met again in Bourdeaux, Alfredo was keen on putting me on the spot to select restaurants for a group of Brazilian colleagues to go out to eat.

Our friends Derek and Ghalib got married during the pandemic and their celebration at the time was restricted because of the constraints. Thus, in October, close to Halloween, they had a redo of the festivities with the great idea of organizing a costume party. We had a blast. Scott, in character, dressed up as a hiker.

In November I went to Bordeaux to attend SBAC 2022. SBAC is a conference that started many years ago in Brazil. It was the first conference that I ever attended and at the time Jairo Panetta was a well-known professor in the area of High-Performance Computing. It was great to reconnect with him in Bordeaux. I also reconnected with several Brazilian colleagues and was designated, by Alfredo Goldman, as the official selector of restaurants.

More than a year ago we joined a group in Edmonton to sponsor a family of refugees from Afghanistan. We got connected with this family because the sister of the head of the family already lived in Edmonton and is a classmate of Oriana, our friend Tema and John's daughter, in medical school. This family had already been prosecuted by the Taliban when they were in power before and were afraid for their lives because they belong to a branch of Islam that believes in educating their daughters. The sponsorship involves a financial commitment as well as other types of support. Their journey was very difficult. They lived for a year in Tajikistan since they fled Afghanistan when the Taliban took over. Our work also involved exerting political pressure on the Canadian government to bring them to Canada when their situation was a very difficult one. This CBC interview gives some background on the situation. Thus, it was with great relief that we were able to welcome the Afghanyar family to Edmonton in November. There are six of them. In December we were able to join them for a dinner cooked by them featuring a delicious Afghan delicacy called Mantu. They are settling in Edmonton. Just a few weeks after arrival some of them already have jobs and others are in school.

As many of the encounters described in this letter are evidence, reconnecting with friends is one of the greatest things about traveling again. Today, December 27, I had another very special moment. Pedro and I go back to 1988 when we were classmates for our M.Sc. program at the Instituto Tecnologico de Aeronautica (ITA). Pedro is one of the special friends with whom I remain close in spite of time and distance. Today I drove down from Ardmore in Oklahoma to Dallas in Texas for a lunch chat with Pedro. Our chat extended for several hours and it was a great feeling to be talking with my attentive friend once again. When I was at ITA Pedro was the one that brought me to Jairo Panetta's seminars at the Instituto Militar the Engenharia (IME). And it was also Pedro who invited me to attend the first SBAC and the first conference of my life in 1988.

Scott was here in Oklahoma the week before Christmas while I stayed home in Edmonton to take care of Junior and work. On Christmas day he went back to Edmonton to go on a ten-day skiing trip to the mountains. I am staying with Daniel here till the 31st and Neil is back home in Edmonton being trained by Junior on how to take care of a smart happy dog.

All the best on the new year.

                Nelson, Scott & Daniel
