, 2023: Adjusting to Reality

2024: Farewall Gaga

This end-of-year letter, also available in Portuguese. Previous editions are found here.

Please reply to jamaral@ualberta.ca.

2024 will be forever marked for Daniel as one of the hardest transitions of his young adult life. He got to stand next to his beloved Grandma Juana as she took her last breath and departed. When Daniel was learning to speak he could not say "Grandma" and instead said "Gaga." The designation stuck in our family. This was before "Lady Gaga" became a phenomenon. Juana was one of Daniel's rocks. Daniel would often call her and spend a long time talking about whatever mattered to Daniel. She would attentively listen and provide her insights. With Juana's passing, the grandparent's home, an important reference anchor for Daniel was sold and his grandpa moved into a new smaller house. It is the end of Christmas traditions that were so important in the first twenty years of Daniel's life.

Late in January, we convinced Daniel to join us for the Department of Computing Science Ski Weekend in Jasper. We took Junior along with us. We are glad that we did because that was the last time that we saw Jasper before the tragic Summer wildfires burned many of the evergreen forests around that region.

When I visited Toronto for some of our research collaborations in February, it was great that we managed to organize a reunion of former and current members of my research group engaged with technology companies in that area. Upon returning to Edmonton, I was pleased that two of my students received awards at the Department Award ceremony: Nathan Henderson received a thesis award, and Quinn Pham received the Gold Medal in Computing Science.

In March my travels took me first to Edinburgh in Scotland, then to Porto Alegre, where we had a great large celebration to commemorate my Mom's 85th birthday. It was the first family reunion after the pandemic. What a great opportunity to see dear nephews and nieces, cousins, friends. Our friends Fred and Gloria traveled from Santos to be with us but caught COVID and could not participate in the celebrations, we met, at a distance, at a park, and outside in the house. My great friend Horacio traveled from Florianopolis and spent a few days with me.

From Porto Alegre I went to Curitiba to coordinate another workshop on communicating scientific ideas and scientific results. It was my first time staying for a couple of weeks in Curitiba. I loved the city. My colleague Marco Zannata was my host at the University there. We had a great group of engaged students for the workshop.

A brief few-days stop in Sao Paulo for me to deliver a seminar version of the communication training at Mackenzie University afforded me an evening to reconnect with my friend Joao Campari, who was my buddy during my years at the University of Texas at Austin. We even danced together!

In April Scott and I drove to Calgary for a weekend to see Scott's hiking buddy Stuart Howe performing the role of Robert Kincaid in a production of the musical The Bridges of Madison County. Stuart is a formally trained Opera Singer and it was a great show. We returned to Calgary in October to see another production in which Stuart was performing. At that time our friends Alejandro and Francisco joined us and we caught up with them over dinner afterwards.

We continue to patronize a great modern ballet company in Edmonton called Ballet Edmonton. In April we had a small group of great friends (John, Tema, Dmitri, Olga, and Rita) joining us to see a joint performance of Ballet Edmonton and the Edmonton Symphony Orchestra at the Winspear Centre. What a treat!

I had a record number of undergraduate students joining our research lab for the Summer between May and August. We started the social activities with a "Junior walk" where Junior invited all the students to join him for a walk down to the dog park. Later in the Summer, we had a few cooking parties and board-game parties at the house to encourage social mingling and have fun together.

Diego Ramalho is a Brazilian dancer with Ballet Edmonton. His parents were visiting from Brazil for the first time and at the end of May we had a great gathering of Brazilian friends to welcome Diego's parents to Edmonton and our house.

The memorial service for Juana Meadows in Ardmore in June allowed us to meet many people in the family and catch up for a longer time with Aunt Diane and her daughter Shelly. A poignant moment was a few days later when Daniel asked to carry his Grandmother's ashes to its final resting place.

In June, one of my trips took me to Porto in Portugal, Copenhagen, Buenos Aires, and Toronto. In Copenhagen, I had a chance to reconnect with a dear Ilmar Santos, a dear Brazilian friend I had not seen in many years. It was great to have dinner with Ilmar and his husband and see a traditional Summer bonfire celebration. In Buenos Aires, I saw Tango shows and a performance of the Buenos Aires Philarmonic Orchestra at Teatro Colon. I also met several friends from the Brazilian Computer Architecture community there. I could not make it to Porto Alegre during that trip because the airport was still closed after the tragic floods of May/June. In Toronto, I caught up with my friend Garrick

In August, my dear friends Artem and Annie made it back to Edmonton and we managed to go together for brunch and a walk in the dog park. I was lucky to catch up with them in San Francisco again in October.

Late in September, Scott, Daniel and I rented a motor home and took a trip down to see the gorgeous views of the Waterton National Park and Glacier National Park.

I served as the general chair for the first academic-led organization of a conference called CASCON which used to be organized by IBM North of Toronto. I brought several students there and it was great for them to meet with my friend Bob Blainey, the creator of the programming language that we used in our compiler course at the University of Alberta.

In November, I met many Brazilian academic colleagues at the Supercomputing conference in Atlanta. Back in Edmonton, Scott and I saw an off-Broadway production of "The Book of Mormon" musical. We also had some great pre-holiday celebrations at friend's houses.

Throughout the year, whenever I was in town to stay with Daniel, Scott took the opportunity to go to the backcountry in the mountains. In the Summer he did several long hikes, often in the company of Stuart Howe. He appears in some of Howe's videos on his youtube channel Howe's the Hike. In the Winter Scott is very active with backcountry skiing. He is involved in organizing ski training for the Edmonton Chapter of the Alpine Club this year.

For Christmas and New Year in 2024 I came to Porto Alegre and Xangrila to visit my Mom and for us to have a family reunion at the old beach house. Daniel and Scott went to Oklahoma to visit with Scott's father, brother, and other members of the family.

All the best in your Winter (or Summer) solstice celebrations.
                Nelson, Scott, Daniel & Junior
